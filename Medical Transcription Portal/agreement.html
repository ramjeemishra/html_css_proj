Business Associate & Confidentiality Agreement

Effective Date: January 1, 2026 · Version 3.2

📄 Rev. 2026 ⚖️ Legally Binding

1. Definitions and Scope

This Agreement is entered into between MediTranscribe Inc. ("Business Associate") and the subscribing hospital or healthcare entity ("Covered Entity"). This agreement governs the handling of Protected Health Information (PHI) as defined under 45 CFR § 160.103.

  • PHI includes any individually identifiable health information transmitted or maintained in any form or medium
  • Electronic PHI (ePHI) refers to PHI that is created, received, maintained, or transmitted electronically
  • Audio recordings containing patient information constitute PHI subject to HIPAA protections

2. Permitted Uses and Disclosures

MediTranscribe may use or disclose PHI only as necessary to perform transcription services on behalf of the Covered Entity, and as required by applicable law.

  • PHI will only be used to perform transcription and quality review services
  • No PHI will be sold, leased, or shared with third parties for any purpose
  • Disclosure is permitted only when required by law or with explicit written consent
  • All transcriptionists sign individual confidentiality agreements

3. Safeguards and Security Obligations

MediTranscribe implements and maintains comprehensive administrative, physical, and technical safeguards in accordance with the HIPAA Security Rule (45 CFR Part 164).

  • AES-256 encryption for all data in transit and at rest
  • Role-based access controls with multi-factor authentication
  • Audit logging of all access to PHI systems
  • Annual security risk assessments and penetration testing
  • Secure deletion of audio files within 30 days of transcript delivery

4. Breach Notification

In the event of a breach of unsecured PHI, MediTranscribe will notify the Covered Entity within 60 days of discovery, as required by the HITECH Act and 45 CFR § 164.410. Notifications will include:

  • Description of what occurred, including the date of the breach
  • Types of PHI involved in the breach
  • Steps MediTranscribe has taken to investigate and mitigate
  • Contact information for affected individuals to ask questions

5. Term and Termination

This Agreement is effective upon the Covered Entity's acceptance and remains in force for the duration of the service relationship. Either party may terminate this Agreement with 30 days written notice. Upon termination, MediTranscribe will return or destroy all PHI within 60 days.

6. Covered Entity Responsibilities

The Covered Entity agrees to the following obligations:

  • Obtain all required patient authorizations prior to uploading audio recordings
  • Notify MediTranscribe of any restrictions on the use or disclosure of specific PHI
  • Report any security incidents involving the MediTranscribe portal to compliance@meditranscribe.com
  • Ensure only authorized users access the hospital portal
